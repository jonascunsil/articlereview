Abstract: Biodegradable and bio-based plastics represent viable alternatives to petroleum-derived synthetic polymers, addressing challenges associated with plastic waste management, carbon emissions, and environmental sustainability. This chapter provides a comprehensive review of recent advancements in bioplastics, bio-based, and biodegradable materials for sustainable food packaging. The objective is to analyze innovations, assess functional properties, and evaluate their applications, safety, and environmental impact. The chapter examines the classification and definition of these materials, followed by a detailed discussion of bioplastics derived from renewable resources. Abstract: Recent advances in the design of food matrices to improve the bioavailability of bioactive food components, such as nutraceuticals and vitamins, are reviewed. Nutraceuticals may be isolated from their natural origin and then incorporated into functional food products as functional ingredients. In this case, specially designed colloidal delivery systems are often needed to improve their dispersibility, stability, food compatibility, and bioavailability. Alternatively, nutraceuticals may be delivered as part of natural foods, such as fruits and vegetables. In this case, their bioavailability can be improved by consuming them with food matrices specifically designed to increase their bioavailability, that is, excipient foods. The utility of a new scheme (NuBACS) for classifying the major factors limiting the oral bioavailability of nutraceuticals is also highlighted.

Abstract: The current study aimed to assess the impact of chitosan coating (0.005 g/mL) combined with thermal treatment (85 °C for 30 min) on tenderness, lipid and protein oxidation, bacterial diversity, and volatile flavor compounds in braised duck leg meat under vacuum packaging during refrigerated storage (4 °C for 15 days). The findings revealed that the three preserved treatments significantly increased tenderness from days 1 to 3. There was a substantial decrease from days 6 to 12 compared to the control, but no significant differences were observed on day 15. Compared with the control, the three preserved treatments reduced TBARS values by 25.8%-78.6% (from days 6 to 12) and total sulfhydryl concentrations by 24.1%-75.7% (from days 3 to 9). The combination treatment had the lowest values (carbonyl concentration, TVC, and TEC) compared to the chitosan coating and thermal treatment, indicating a significant synergistic effect. The proportions of the four primary spoilage organisms, Brochothrix, Weissella, Acinetobacter, and Pseudomonas, were 74.8%, 76.3%, 70.7%, and 49.7% in control, chitosan coating, thermal treatment, and combination treatment, respectively. The combination treatment produced the most volatile flavor compounds (38 compounds) at the end of storage (15 days). Hexanal, 1-nonanal, 1-octen-3-ol, and 2, 3-octanedione were the main volatile flavor compounds, and the average relative peak area was above 80. Therefore, chitosan coating and thermal treatment could be developed as synergistic methods to preserve braised duck meat.

Abstract: There has been a mounting interest in research on natural antioxidants with promising bioactive properties; however, studies on macromolecular antioxidants are still scanty. Proteins, polysaccharides, and nonextractable polyphenols are well-acknowledged as macromolecules with excellent antioxidant properties. As evidenced by research work, these macromolecules can effectively scavenge reactive oxygen species and control oxidative stress, which leads to various pathogenesis. Plant, animal, marine, and microbial sources are identified as major natural sources of bioactive macromolecules. The surge of studies carried out in the area of macromolecules has confirmed the potential applications of these bioactive molecules in the food industry as a food additive, functional food, and food packaging material. Moreover, the potential applications of these macromolecules also extend toward the pharmaceutical, cosmeceutical, and biomedical industries.
